About the Spinone Italiano

The Spinone Italiano is a large Italian hunting dog known for its calm temperament, loyal personality, and distinctive shaggy coat. Originally bred to track, point, and retrieve game.

History

The Spinone Italiano is one of the oldest pointing dog breeds in Europe. Dogs similar to the Spinone existed in Italy more than 2000 years ago.

During the Renaissance, rough-coated hunting dogs similar to the Spinone appeared in Italian paintings. These dogs were valued for their endurance and ability to work in forests, mountains and marshes.

The breed almost disappeared during World War II, but Italian breeders rebuilt the population in the 1950s.
