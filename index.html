About the Spinone Italiano

The Spinone Italiano is a large Italian hunting dog known for its calm temperament, loyal personality, and distinctive shaggy coat. Originally bred to track, point, and retrieve game, it is one of the most versatile hunting dogs in Europe.

Despite its strong working abilities, the Spinone is famous for its gentle nature and strong bond with its human family. Today the breed is both a capable sporting dog and a loving companion.

History

The Spinone Italiano is one of the oldest pointing dog breeds in Europe. Dogs resembling the Spinone can be traced back more than 2,000 years, with ancestors believed to have existed as early as 500 B.C. in ancient Italy.

During the Renaissance, rough-coated hunting dogs similar to the Spinone appeared in Italian paintings and writings. These dogs were valued by hunters for their endurance, intelligence, and ability to work in difficult terrain such as forests, mountains, and marshes.

The Spinone was bred as a versatile hunting dog capable of tracking, pointing, and retrieving game on both land and in water. Unlike faster pointing breeds, the Spinone worked methodically and carefully, relying on its excellent sense of smell and steady pace to locate game.

The breed suffered greatly during World War II, when many dogs were lost and the population declined significantly. After the war, dedicated breeders began rebuilding the breed in the 1950s, restoring the Spinone’s traditional appearance and hunting abilities.

Today the Spinone Italiano is appreciated not only as a capable hunting dog but also as a loyal family companion known for its calm and affectionate nature.

Temperament

Friendly

Spinoni are affectionate and sociable dogs that enjoy human company.

Intelligent

They are intelligent and trainable, although sometimes a little stubborn.

Family Dog

Spinoni bond strongly with their families and are usually great with children.

Active

They love outdoor activities like hiking, swimming and exploring.

Care & Grooming

Exercise

Spinoni are active dogs that need daily walks and outdoor activities. They enjoy long hikes and exploring nature.

Grooming

Their rough coat requires brushing several times a week. Their beard may also need regular cleaning after meals.

Health

Spinoni are generally healthy dogs but may be prone to hip dysplasia and certain eye conditions.

Training

Positive reinforcement works best when training this intelligent but sometimes stubborn breed.

Fun Facts

  • The Spinone has a distinctive beard and moustache.
  • The breed is an excellent swimmer.
  • The name "Spinone" comes from the Italian word for thorny bushes.
  • They are often called "gentle giants".
  • Spinoni are sometimes used as therapy dogs.
